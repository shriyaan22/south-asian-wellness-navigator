Foundational Academic Citations

These papers inform the culturally grounded, holistic approach of the South Asian Well-Being Navigator, particularly concerning the concepts of idioms of distress and the social determinants of health.

  1. Nichter, M. (1981). Idioms of distress: Alternatives in the expression of psychosocial distress: A case study from South India. Culture, Medicine and Psychiatry, 5(4), 379–408.
    DOI Link to Abstract / PDF Access

    *Note: This is a seminal paper that introduced the "Idioms of Distress" concept, based on fieldwork in South India.*

  2. Bhatia, M., & Degiovanni, N. (2018). The mental health of South Asian immigrants and their descendants. Advances in Psychiatry and Behavioral Health, 1(1), 32-41.
    Link to PubMed Central (Full Text)

    *Note: Discusses high rates of mental health disorders and acculturative stress in South Asian immigrant populations.*

  3. Weaver, L. D. (2017). Tension Among Women in North India: An Idiom of Distress and a Cultural Syndrome. Culture, Medicine, and Psychiatry, 41(1), 58-86.
    Link to PDF

    *Note: Provides a specific, modern example of a common idiom of distress ("tension") within a South Asian context.*

  4. Kirmayer, L. J., & Groleau, D. (2001). Cultural models of the self and emotion: Implications for psychiatric research and practice. American Psychiatric Association Annual Review, 20, 127–167.
    Link to ResearchGate

    *Note: Provides the theoretical context for understanding how culture shapes self-concept and emotional expression.*
